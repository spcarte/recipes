# Grandma's Christmas Cookies
These are Grandma Carter's christmas cookies. They are basically just sugar cookies, but this is specifically the recipe that was used in the family christmas cookie exchange.

## Ingredients 

- 2.5 cups all purpose flour
- 1.5 teaspoons baking powder
- 0.75 teaspoons salt
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon
- 1 cup white sugar
- 0.75 cups vegetable oil
- 2 large eggs
- 1 teaspoon vanilla 

```{note}
This recipe just covers the cookies. A separate recipe should be used for the frosting.
```
```{tip}
Make sure to purchase the cookie decorating supplies when getting the ingredients. This could include colored sugar, sprinkles, cinnamon dots, food coloring, etc. Make sure to get the "normal" food colorings instead of neon food colorings.
```

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees fahrenheit 
2. Sift together:
    - Flour
    - Baking powder
    - Salt
    - Cinnamon
3. In a separate bowl:
    - First combine:
        - Sugar
        - Vegetable oil
    - Add:
        - Eggs
        - Vanilla
    - Mix everything together very well 
4. Roll cookie batter onto a floured surface to a thickness of ~0.125-0.25", then cut to the desired shape with a cookie cutter
```{tip}
Make sure that the dough is firm enough to easily move between surfaces. It may be necessary to add more flour to make it firmer and easier to move.
```
5. Move the cut cookies to a lightly greased cookie sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes
```{note} 
Colored sugar should be put on before baking if that's the decorating method.
```
5. Remove from the oven when edges of the cookies are slightly firm but not brown, they will continue cooking when removed from the oven 
6. Immediately remove the cookies from the cookie sheet and let them cool on a rack before decorating 

## Extra Information
```{tip}
Dough should be firm but not hard. It is better to start with a wet dough because flour can be added to dry it out. 
```
```{warning}
Never add a third egg to the dough.
```
