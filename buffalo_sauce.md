# Buffalo Sauce
This is a buttery buffalo sauce that is good on pork and chicken. The spice level is easily modified by changing the hot sauce mixture. 

## Ingredients 

- 1/2 cup butter (one stick)
- 1/2 cup hot sauce, the majority should be Franks Red Hot but other sauces can be used to make the hot sauce spicer or change the flavor profile
```{tip}
1-2 table spoons for a high heat (e.g., carolina reaper or ghost pepper) hot sauce will make an "extra hot" sauce.
```
- 1.5 teaspoons ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 0.125 teaspoons xanthan gum 

## Instructions

The butter is melted in a double boiler, which can either be a true double boiler or a metal bowl that is placed on top of a small sauce pan. If using a bowl and sauce pan, make sure that they fit together well enough so plenty of water can be added to the sauce pan without touching the bottom of the bowl. 

1. Set-up to cook the sauce
    - Pre-portion the spices and xanthan gum into a small bowl
    - Portion the hot sauce mixture
    - Cut the butter into small pads and place them in a metal bowl or top part of the double boiler
2. Add water to the sauce pan (or double boiler) and bring to a boil
3. Place the metal bowl over the sauce pan and melt the butter, regularly mix the butter to prevent it from splitting
4. Mix the spices into the butter as soon as it is melted, immediately remove the mixture from heat
5. Add the hot sauce mixture to the melted butter (right away after the butter is removed from heat) and vigorously whisk the mixture
6. Let the sauce cool for ~10-15 minutes before using it, occasionally mix the sauce while it is cooling

## Extra Information 
The black pepper reduces the acidity of the buffalo sauce, which makes it taste less vinegary. 

```{tip}
The spices dissolve into the sauce better when mixed into the butter prior to the hot sauce. This recipe seems to take a max of 3 teaspoons of spice before it gets gritty. 
```
```{warning}
Be careful about the amount of xanthan gum. Too much and the sauce will turn to gelatine, too little and the sauce will split. 
```
