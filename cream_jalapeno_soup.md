# Cream of Jalapeno Soup
This soup combines a creamy, spicey, and "fresh" flavor that is a nice edition to any meal. It likely isn't filling enough to be a meal in and of itself. 

## Ingredients

- 8 medium jalapenos
- 1 medium yellow onion
- 4 cloves garlic (use more or less to taste)
- 0.5 cups cilantro
- 1-2 tablespoons lime juice
- 2 cups heavy cream
- 2 cups frozen corn (steam bag corn works well)
- 2 cups chicken stock
- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
```{note}
More butter can be added (up to 8 tablespoons) to make the soup have a richer flavor.
```
- 4 tablespoons Wondra (an instant/quick mixing flour)
```{note}
Regular flour can be used instead of Wondra, but it will be more difficult to make the roux.
```
- 2 tablespoons pepper
- 0.5 tablespoons salt

```{tip}
This soup can be made spicier by adding chile powder when the pepper and salt is added. Adding more jalapenos to the recipe may mess with the flavor balance.
```

## Instructions

1. Set-up to cook the soup
    - Finely chop/mince the jalapenos and onions and set aside in a large bowl
    - Mince the garlic and set aside in a small bowl
    - Combine the spices into a small bowl
    - Pre-portion the chicken broth in a bowl
    - Pre-portion the Wondra in a bowl
    - Cook the corn, then toast it in a large pan with oil, this can usually be done during step two
    - Set aside the heavy cream so it is nearer to room temperature when it is added to the soup
2. Melt the butter in a medium pot over medium heat
3. Caramelize the jalapenos and onions occasionally stirring to prevent burning
```{tip}
This will take ~30 minutes and the jalapenos will loose a lot of color during the process. Pay attention later in the process since the onion and jalapeno will be very near burning.
```
4. Add the garlic and seasoning while continuously mixing to "open up" the flavors
5. Add the flour and cook until it is browned, some should stick to the bottom of the pot
6. Gradually pour and mix in the chicken broth
```{tip}
It is helpful to do this in multiple steps so the "cold" chicken broth can lift the stuck flour off the bottom of the pot.
```
7. Simmer the mixture until it has thickened (10-15 minutes)
8. Add the cream and lime juice and simmer until it has thickened (5-10 minutes)
```{tip}
Now is a good time to taste the soup for flavor and add chile powder to increase the spice level.
```
9. Mix in the toasted corn
10. Chop the cilantro and add to the soup, immediately remove the soup from heat after adding the cilantro

## Extra Information
```{warning}
Do not chop the onions too finely. The 'onion juice" cannot be caramelized and will impart too much fresh onion flavor to the soup. 
```
